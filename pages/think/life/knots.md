## Taut-line hitch
- The best knot to tie two ends together into an adjustable, sliding loop is the ==**Scout Knot**== (often called the **Adjustable Grip Hitch** or **Taut-Line Hitch** variant). 
- It creates a secure loop that you can easily slide up and down the standing line to make it larger or smaller.
- This is the one used on ten guy ropes
- [The taut-line hitch - video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H4rbBHp1QXo)
# Making a loop tighter
## Trucker's hitch
- After joining the ropes into one, you can make the whole line shorter and apply serious tension later by creating a Trucker's Hitch. This allows you to pull the rope tight, lock it in place, and adjust it at any time.
- Create a loop in the middle of your combined rope (an alpine butterfly or a simple overhand loop will work).
- Wrap the far end of your long rope around a secure anchor point (like a tree or a hook).
- Thread that same end through the middle-loop you created, pull firmly to draw the rope tight (acting as a pulley system), and secure it with a couple of half-hitches around the main line.
- [See video here](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/zvUB6AGWHQ0)
